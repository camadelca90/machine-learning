Unsupervised Learning & K-Means Clustering

1. What is Unsupervised Learning?

Definition: Unsupervised learning extracts patterns from data without labeled responses. The algorithm discovers hidden insights autonomously.

Unlike supervised learning (input-output pairs), unsupervised works with raw, unannotated data. Essential for exploratory analysis, dimension reduction, and clustering.

Fundamental principle: "Let the data speak for itself" — natural groupings emerge without answers.
Fact: Over 80% of real-world data is unlabeled — critical for finance, healthcare, e-commerce.

3. What is K-Means?

Definition: Iterative centroid-based algorithm partitioning n points into K clusters. Each point belongs to the cluster with the nearest centroid.

"K" = number of clusters, "Means" = centroids as mean of points.

Objective: Minimize WCSS
J = Σj=1K Σxᵢ∈Sⱼ ||xᵢ - μⱼ||²
Why Popular
  • ✓ Simple, easy to implement
  • ✓ Scales well O(n·K·iter)
  • ✓ Guaranteed convergence
  • ✓ Core for compression & quantization
Limitations
  • ⚠️ Sensitive to initialization
  • ⚠️ Requires K in advance
  • ⚠️ Assumes spherical clusters
  • ⚠️ Outlier sensitive

4. Centroids

Definition: Geometric center of a cluster = multidimensional mean of all points in that cluster.

μj = (1/|Sj|) Σxᵢ∈Sj xᵢ
  • • Minimizes sum of squared distances
  • • Moves iteratively during reassignment
  • • Convergence when centroids stop moving
Initialization Strategies
RandomSimple but poor local minima
K-Means++Spreads centroids → better quality
ManualDomain knowledge
Example (2D): Points (2,3), (4,5), (6,7) → Centroid = (4,5)

5. Distance Metrics & Euclidean

Distance metric: Quantifies similarity between points. Euclidean distance = straight-line distance (Pythagorean).

d(p,q) = √[ Σ (pᵢ - qᵢ)² ]
Squared Euclidean (WCSS): Σ (pᵢ - qᵢ)²
Euclidean Properties
  • Symmetry: d(p,q) = d(q,p)
  • Positivity: ≥0, zero only if equal
  • Triangle inequality

Why Euclidean? The mean minimizes squared Euclidean distances.

K-Means alternates between assignment and update until convergence. Each iteration reduces WCSS.

7. Variance & Convergence (WCSS)

WCSS = Within-Cluster Sum of Squares = total intra-cluster variance. The core objective K-Means minimizes.

WCSS = Σj=1K Σxᵢ∈Cⱼ ||xᵢ - μⱼ||²

Lower WCSS = tighter clusters. Use elbow method to choose optimal K.

WCSS decreases across iterations

Elbow at K=3 → optimal clusters
