📊 Understanding the dataset

Customer segmentation based on Age & Income (COP)

Original data source

The dataset is a synthetic representation of 100 customers with demographic and financial information. It was originally built within the Excel file Iteraciones.xlsx to demonstrate the K-Means clustering algorithm step by step over three iterations.

🎯 Purpose: Segment customers into meaningful groups based on their age and monthly income to design targeted marketing strategies.

Key preprocessing
  • No missing values — complete records.
  • No scaling applied intentionally to keep real values (Income in COP).
  • Euclidean distance computed directly between raw features.
  • Centroids initialized with strategic seeds: C1 (22yo, 1M COP), C2 (45yo, 5.5M COP), C3 (60yo, 8M COP).

Key insight
Best clustering at Iteration 2

After first initialization (C1: young/low income, C2: mid, C3: senior/high), the second iteration drastically reduced intra-cluster variance (WCSS ≈ 44.78 × 10^12). The third iteration shows a spike due to centroid rebalancing — typical K-Means behavior.
