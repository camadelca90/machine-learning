MACHINE LEARNING CASE STUDY EMAIL FILTERING

¿What is machine learning email filtering?

It is a system that uses machine learning algorithms to classify emails into predefined categories (such as spam, primary, social, promotional) or detect unwanted content, without the need for manually programmed rules.

¿How does it work?

A set of pre-labeled emails (e.g., "spam" or "non-spam", "promotional" or "primary") is used for training. The data is processed to extract relevant features, such as:

  • Frequently used words or phrases
  • Sender and email address
  • Message format (use of capital letters, links, attachments)
  • Date and time sent
  • Subject line structure

Model training

A machine learning algorithm is selected and trained. The most commonly used are:

  • Naive Bayes: Simple and efficient, very common for text classification
  • Decision Trees and Random Forests: Good for identifying complex patterns
  • SVM (Support Vector Machines): Effective in binary classification problems (spam/no spam)
  • Neural Networks: For cases with large volumes of data and complex features

Classification and continuous improvement

Once trained, the model classifies new incoming emails. Furthermore, the systems typically include a feedback mechanism: when the user marks an email as "spam" or corrects its classification, that information is used to retrain and refine the model, improving its accuracy over time.

  • Spam detection
  • Categorization
  • Malicious content detection

Advantages

  • Greater accuracy: Adapts to new types of spam or messages as they appear
  • Fewer false positives: Reduces the risk of marking important emails as spam
  • Automation: No manual keyword list updates required
  • Scalability: Works efficiently with large email volumes

Challenges

  • Spam evolution: Spammers change their techniques to avoid detection
  • Languages and dialects: Difficulty adapting to different languages or linguistic variations
  • False negatives: Spam emails that go undetected
  • Privacy: Processing email content can raise concerns about user privacy

Examples of platforms that use it

  • Gmail (Google)
  • Outlook (Microsoft)
  • Yahoo Mail
  • Business email services such as IBM Notes or Zoho Mail
