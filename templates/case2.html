Sales Forecasting in a Store

Monthly Sales Forecasting

Problem description: Businesses need to estimate in advance how many products they will sell in a given period, such as weeks or months. This is important because poor planning can lead to financial losses: too little inventory results in lost sales; too much inventory can lead to storage costs or unsold products. Machine Learning can be used to analyze historical data to identify customer behavior patterns, such as peak seasons (e.g., December or special promotions). With this information, the model can help the business anticipate future demand and make better strategic decisions.

Type of Machine Learning: Supervised Learning (Regression), since the model learns to predict continuous numerical values ​​from previously labeled historical data.

Input data:

  • Sales history from previous months or years
  • Product prices
  • Applied promotions and discounts
  • Seasons of the year (Christmas, holidays, etc.)
  • Store location
  • Market trends
  • Customer purchasing behavior

Expected output:

A numerical prediction of the quantity of products likely to be sold in a specific period (e.g., next month). The system can display estimated values ​​to help plan the necessary inventory.

Real-world relevance:

This type of model is widely used in supermarkets, online stores, and distribution companies. It allows for the reduction of financial losses, optimization of the quantity of products stored, and improved production and logistics planning. Furthermore, it helps companies improve customer satisfaction, as products will be available when needed
